Overview

"And God said: Let there be lights made in the firmament of heaven, to divide the day and the night, and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days and years:"Gen 1:14

This clock measures time using 24 unequal hours each solar day. The hours are unequal in the sense that in summer, ticks above the horizon represent more time than ticks below, while the opposite is true for winter.

"The heavens shew forth the glory of God, and the firmament declareth the work of his hands."Ps 19:1

The horizon of the clockface represents the Horizon of the selected location. Each day, the Sun indicator rises and sets with the Sun, and likewise for the Moon (with her phases). The Zodiac also follows its daily course, and the position of the Sun and Moon in relation to the Zodiac is also shown. Over the course of the year, the Sun is shown progressing through the Seasons, both astronomical and liturgical.
